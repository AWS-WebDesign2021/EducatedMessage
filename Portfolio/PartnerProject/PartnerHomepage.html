Background

The internet is being used for countless purposes on infinite websites and apps, by billions of people worldwide. For
many reasons, 86% of internet users have made at least some kind of attempt to make themselves somewhat anonymous
when they are online. Anonymity, in the sense of online users, is “any interaction a user has on the Internet that protects
his or her identity from being shared with another user or with a third party.” Depending on the intentions of the
anonymous user and the reason they want to stay anonymous, this tool can be incredibly helpful and incredibly harmful
depending on how it is used. From organizing protests and becoming part of an online community to cyberbullying and
scams, these pages will highlight the main pros and cons of online anonymity.


This was my first and only time making a webpage with a partner. The topic was Social and Ethical Issues of IT, and my partner, Sofia, and I chose anonymity. While working with Sofia was really fun and having another person’s experience, style and perspective was super helpful, it was a little bit harder because we had to upload new versions and switch computers and coordinate edit times, which I had never dealt with before. I also think that because we had different styles, trying to communicate what we wanted and compromise was a little clumsy. In the end, I learned a lot about communication and about coding, thanks to Sofia, but I was definitely a bit burnt out by the end. One specific problem I had was that my hyperlinks were purple because I had already clicked on them and my background was also purple, so I spent over an hour trying to figure out why my links aren't showing up, just to find out that everything was fine and they just happened to blend in with my background.
